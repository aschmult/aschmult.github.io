My Journey at Villanova

When I arrived at Villanova, I wasn't exactly sure what field of business I was most interested in. As time went on, and I got involved in activities around campus and explored careers through my core business classes, I discovered that my interests lie at the intersection of business and technology.

I'm planning to declare majors in Management Information Systems and Business Analytics, and I have already declared a minor in Computer Science. The courses for these programs have some overlapping content, so I'm excited to be able to apply what I learn in one course to what I am learnin in another.

VSB has offered me a wider variety of resources to help me prepare for professional success, both through classes and the Clay Center. One of the most useful resources has been the VSB Majors Fair, which I attended early in the fall of my sophomore year. The Majors Fair gave me the opportunity to talk to professionals in various fields, and learn the wide variety of careers a given major could prepare me for. I was quite surpirsed at the applications of some degrees. For instance, I learned that the FBI hired accountants to investigate fraud, money laundering, and other financial crimes.

My Career Plans

Although I'm interested in the entire intersection of business and technology, one field that interests me in particular is database management. My experiences with extracurriculars have given me the chance to create and manage a database, and I have enjoyed the challenge of figuring out how to best collect, store, and retrieve data.
