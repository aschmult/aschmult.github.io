Experience

Villanova University Tech Crew

Tech Crew is Villanova's student run event production business. We provide audio, lighting, and video support for events around campus, from guest speakers to large-scale concerts. In 2018, we had revenue of over $60,000, and worked 153 events.

I joined Tech Crew about a month after starting school at Villanova. During my first semester, I worked as an Event Staff and a Senior Staff member, and I took my Head Technician test in December. As a Head Technician, I am responsible for running client events. This includes managing a group of 3-8 staff, coordinating with clients, and troubleshooting technical issues.

At the start of 2019, I joined the Executive Board of Tech Crew as the Events Manager. As Events Manager, I take client specifications for events, and develop a plan for equipment and staffing. For large scale events, I attend production meetings and coordinate equipment rentals. Additionally, as problems with staffing or equipment arise, I work with other Executive Board members to find solutions.

My Website

This website was created as a personal project during the summer of 2019, while I was abroad interning at Better Trader. The website is written entirely from scratch, with help from w3schools and StackOverflow, and a lot of time working with a hex color picker and a css gradient maker. It is hosted through GitHub, and you can view the repository, which includes the current website, old versions, and some files that are me just playing around.

The current site is the third version I have created. The original version of the site was fairly primitive and not written with best practices in mind, and was not at all mobile responsive. The second version was created to be somewhat similar to the current site, with a background and white cards, but was never quite finished. I intended to add functionality to have one card displayed on the first slide, but never got around to writing the JavaScript. In addition, using an image as the background slowed down the loading of the site.

I created the current version of the site shortly after beginning to work on the Professional Identity project mentioned above. The original design for that project was plain white with grey lines dividing sections, but I decided to do something with some color, and I got the idea to do a gradient background with white cards, and I liked the style enough that I decided to re-do my website in the same style.
